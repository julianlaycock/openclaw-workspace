The AIFMD II Annex IV XML Schema, Explained for Humans

200+ fields. Conditional logic. NCA-specific quirks. Here's what the schema actually says — in language you can forward to your team.

What Is Annex IV and Why Should You Care

Annex IV is the standardized reporting template that every AIFM in the EU must file with its National Competent Authority under the Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive. AIFMD II — the revised directive entering force on April 16, 2026 — significantly expands what you need to report: more granular exposure data, new leverage breakdowns, additional risk metrics, and tighter validation rules. The filing format is XML, validated against ESMA's XSD schema (currently Rev 6, version 1.2).

If you're a compliance officer at a German KVG, this is not optional reading. Your first filing under the new regime could be due as early as Q2 2026, and the XML schema has over 200 fields with conditional logic that will reject your submission if you get a single enumeration wrong. The good news: once you understand the structure, it's not magic — it's just bureaucracy with angle brackets.

The XML Schema in Plain Language

The Annex IV XML file is a nested tree. Think of it as a form with sections. Here's the hierarchy that actually matters:

1. AIFMReportingInfo — the wrapper

The root element. Contains metadata about who's filing and when.

<AIFMReportingInfo
  CreationDateAndTime="2026-06-30T12:00:00"
  Version="1.2"
  ReportingMemberState="DE">

This tells ESMA: "This report was created on this date, uses schema version 1.2, and is filed in Germany."

2. AIFMRecordInfo — about the manager

One per AIFM. This section identifies your firm:

<AIFMRecordInfo>
  <FilingType>INIT</FilingType>
  <AIFMContentType>1</AIFMContentType>
  <ReportingPeriodStartDate>2026-01-01</ReportingPeriodStartDate>
  <ReportingPeriodEndDate>2026-03-31</ReportingPeriodEndDate>
  <ReportingPeriodType>Q1</ReportingPeriodType>
  <AIFMJurisdiction>DE</AIFMJurisdiction>
  <AIFMNationalCode>K-FR-XXXX-XXXX-X</AIFMNationalCode>
  <AIFMName>Muster KVG mbH</AIFMName>
  <AIFMEEAFlag>true</AIFMEEAFlag>
  <!-- ... -->
</AIFMRecordInfo>

Key fields: FilingType (INIT for first filing, AMND for amendments), AIFMContentType (1 = full report with AIF data, 2 = AIFM-only), and the ReportingPeriod block that defines which quarter you're filing.

3. AIFRecordInfo — about each fund

One per AIF managed. This is where most of the complexity lives:

<AIFRecordInfo>
  <AIFNationalCode>DE000EXAMPLE01</AIFNationalCode>
  <AIFName>Muster Immobilienfonds I</AIFName>
  <AIFDescription>
    <PredominantAIFType>PEQF</PredominantAIFType>
    <AIFBaseCurrency>EUR</AIFBaseCurrency>
    <AIFNetAssetValue>250000000</AIFNetAssetValue>
  </AIFDescription>
  <AIFIndividualInfo>
    <!-- Exposures, leverage, risk — the deep stuff -->
  </AIFIndividualInfo>
</AIFRecordInfo>

Inside AIFIndividualInfo, you'll find blocks for MainInstrumentsTraded (top 5 instruments by value), IndividualExposure (macro and geographical breakdowns), RiskProfile (market, counterparty, liquidity), LeverageInfo (Gross and Commitment methods), StressTests (new in AIFMD II), and LiquidityManagement tools (also new).

4. The exposure & risk blocks

These are tables encoded as XML. Each row is an element with coded values:

<MainInstrumentTraded>
  <Ranking>1</Ranking>
  <SubAssetType>DER_FEX_FORW</SubAssetType>
  <InstrumentCodeType>ISIN</InstrumentCodeType>
  <InstrumentValue>45000000</InstrumentValue>
</MainInstrumentTraded>

The sub-asset type codes (DER_FEX_FORW = derivative, foreign exchange, forward) are enumerated in the XSD. There are roughly 80 of them. Get one character wrong and validation fails — sometimes silently on certain NCA portals.

5 Fields That Trip Everyone Up

1. LEI Validation

The AIFMLEICode and AIFLEICode fields must contain a valid, active 20-character LEI. Not "applied for." Not expired. Not the old pre-LEI code from 2019. ESMA's validator cross-checks against the GLEIF database. If your LEI lapsed and you didn't renew it, your entire filing gets rejected — not just the field.

Fix: Check your LEI status at search.gleif.org before filing season. Renewal takes 1–3 business days. Don't leave this for filing week.

2. ReportingMemberState

This isn't where your fund is domiciled. It's where the AIFM is authorized. German KVG managing a Luxembourg fund? ReportingMemberState is DE, not LU. Mixing this up means your file goes to the wrong NCA — or gets rejected by the right one.

3. NAVFrequency

ESMA expects specific codes: D (daily), W (weekly), M (monthly), Q (quarterly), HY (half-yearly), Y (yearly). Common mistake: using A for annual (it's Y) or S for semi-annual (it's HY). These are enumerated in the XSD — there's no freetext fallback.

4. PredominantAIFType

You must classify each fund: HFND (hedge fund), PEQF (private equity), REST (real estate), FOFS (fund of funds), OTHR (other). The gotcha: this drives which subsequent fields are required. Classify a real estate fund as OTHR and you'll skip required property-type breakdowns. Classify it correctly as REST and 15 more fields appear. There's no shortcut — pick the right type from the start.

5. Leverage: Gross vs. Commitment Method

AIFMD II requires leverage reported under both methods. These are not the same number:

<LeverageAIF>
  <GrossMethodRate>2.35</GrossMethodRate>
  <CommitmentMethodRate>1.80</CommitmentMethodRate>
</LeverageAIF>

A ratio of 1.0 means no leverage. If your Gross is lower than your Commitment, something is wrong with your calculation. Common error: submitting percentages (235%) instead of ratios (2.35).

NCA Differences That Matter

The XSD is European. The filing portals are national. Each NCA adds its own flavor.

BaFin (Germany)

  • Portal: MVP Portal (Meldewesen-Plattform)
  • National code format: BaFin uses its own WM-Nummer / registration code. Must match their records exactly.
  • Extra validation: BaFin runs plausibility checks beyond XSD. A schema-valid file can still be rejected if NAV figures are inconsistent across sections.
  • Frequency quirk: Sub-threshold AIFMs file annually, but the reporting period must align to calendar quarters — you file once, for Q4.

CSSF (Luxembourg)

  • Portal: eDesk (SOFiE module)
  • LEI mandatory since 2024 — no grace period. Placeholder LEIs are hard-rejected under AIFMD II.
  • Passporting fields: German KVG with a Lux-domiciled AIF? CSSF expects AIFMemberState = LU and ReportingMemberState = DE. Getting these crossed is the #1 support ticket at CSSF.

AMF (France)

  • Portal: GECO / ROSA
  • Stricter on stress tests — AMF expects populated stress test fields even in transitional periods where other NCAs accept blanks.
  • Heads up: Error messages are in French. Plan accordingly.
Universal truth: No two NCA portals give the same error messages for the same problem. BaFin gives you a plausibility code. CSSF gives you a line number. AMF gives you a French sentence. Build your validation locally before uploading anywhere.

How to Validate Your XML

Layer 1: XSD Validation

The minimum. Validate against ESMA's official XSD (Rev 6 v1.2). This catches missing required elements, wrong enumeration values, invalid data types, and structural nesting errors. Any XML editor or CLI tool (xmllint, Python's lxml) handles this.

Layer 2: Business Logic Checks

XSD won't catch:

  • NAV values that don't add up across sections
  • Leverage ratios below 1.0 (mathematically impossible)
  • Exposure percentages that don't sum to ~100%
  • Missing sections conditionally required by PredominantAIFType

This is where most rejections happen. Technically valid XML, logically broken.

Layer 3: NCA-Specific Pre-Checks

Each NCA has quirks beyond the standard. Ideally, validate against your specific NCA's known rules before uploading to their portal.
