Mix 1 takes B's compliance score as the anchor — one glance tells you "78, review needed." KPIs sit beside it in a single row so the entire top strip is your executive summary. Below that, D's sticky sidebar keeps actions, risk flags, and deadlines always visible while you scroll through funds → analytics → decisions on the left. The score creates emotional clarity ("are we OK?"), the sidebar creates operational persistence ("what's broken?"), and the main column is where you investigate. This is the "executive who also does the work" layout.

Mix 2 is tighter and more opinionated. The compliance score is inlined into the header row — no dedicated card, just a small ring next to the title. This saves vertical space and makes the score feel like metadata rather than a hero element. The sidebar goes deeper: it adds a Fund Health summary (traffic-light per fund with flag counts) so you can see cross-fund status without scrolling. Action Queue items are grouped by severity with section headers. Deadlines show countdown badges ("23d"). The 3rd fund collapses to a compact row (from Option C's pattern) since it's low-activity. Analytics get a 2×2 grid instead of 3-col for denser information. This is the "I live in this dashboard 8 hours a day" layout — maximum information density without clutter.
