# AIFMD II National Transposition: Comprehensive Market Opportunity Report

**Last Updated:** 24 February 2026  
**Directive:** (EU) 2024/927 – "AIFMD II"  
**Transposition Deadline:** 16 April 2026 (≈51 days away)  
**New Reporting Requirements:** Apply from 16 April 2027

---

## 🔥 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: BIGGEST OPPORTUNITY WINDOWS

### The Headline
AIFMD II (Directive 2024/927) entered into force on 15 April 2024. **All 27 EU Member States must transpose it into national law by 16 April 2026** — just ~7 weeks from now. As of late February 2026, **only Germany, Luxembourg, and the Netherlands have published draft implementing legislation**. Countries like Belgium, France, Italy, and Spain have NOT yet published drafts. This creates a massive compliance scramble across the EU.

### Key Dates
| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 26 March 2024 | AIFMD II published in EU Official Journal |
| 15 April 2024 | Entry into force |
| **16 April 2026** | **Transposition deadline — all Member States must have national law** |
| 16 April 2027 | New Annex IV reporting requirements apply (Art. 24 AIFMD, Art. 20a UCITS) |

### Top 5 Opportunity Windows

1. **🇪🇸 Spain (CNMV)** — No draft legislation published. Historically late transposer (was warned by EC in 2014 for AIFMD I delays). CNMV ecosystem underserved by RegTech. Lanzadera connection = direct market entry.

2. **🇫🇷 France (AMF)** — AMF has updated doctrine on liquidity management tools, but no draft transposition bill published. ~700+ AIFMs need compliance tools. AMF ecosystem is sophisticated but fragmented.

3. **🇮🇹 Italy (CONSOB/Banca d'Italia)** — No draft legislation published. Historically slow transposer. Smaller RegTech vendor presence compared to LU/IE.

4. **🇧🇪 Belgium (FSMA)** — No draft legislation published. Mid-size market but proximity to EU institutions creates regulatory pressure.

5. **Annex IV Reporting Overhaul (All Markets)** — ESMA standardized templates won't be final until April 2027, but firms must prepare NOW. The shift from national formats to EU-standardized XML creates a massive tool/platform need. Current vendors (SS&C, FE fundinfo, Matterhorn RS, ARKK, anevis, DataTracks) will need to update — window for new entrants.

### The Compliance Gap
- **51 days** until transposition deadline
- **~14 months** until new Annex IV reporting obligations
- Many countries haven't even published draft legislation
- ESMA's RTS on liquidity management tools adopted Nov 2025, but compliance not mandatory until April 2027
- RTS on open-ended loan-originating funds: EC confirmed won't be formally adopted before 1 October 2027
- **Fund managers are confused** — multiple overlapping deadlines, unclear national rules

### 🇩🇪 GERMANY (BaFin) — Our Home Market
**Status:** 🟢 Draft published  
**NCA:** BaFin (Bundesanstalt für Finanzdienstleistungsaufsicht)  
**Key Law:** KAGB (Kapitalanlagegesetzbuch)

**Transposition Progress:**
- Germany published the **Fondsrisikobegrenzungsgesetz (FRiG)** — Fund Risk Limitation Act — as draft legislation in September 2025
- Largely literal transposition of AIFMD II into KAGB
- Key changes: new loan origination framework for AIFs, updated delegation rules
- Small AIFMs (KVG light/sAIFM) will see significant changes — loan origination restrictions lifted
- Expected to meet 16 April 2026 deadline

**Annex IV Reporting:**
- BaFin requires XML files per ESMA format (ESMA 2013/1358 schema, now rev 6)
- Submission via **MVP-Portal** (BaFin's reporting and publishing platform)
- No other form of transmission permitted
- Germany has additional national reporting via KAGB §35

**Market Size:** ~600+ registered AIFMs. Major market.

**Opportunity:**
- FRiG creates new compliance needs especially for small AIFMs entering loan origination
- BaFin's MVP-Portal is functional but not user-friendly — tool opportunity for portal interface/automation
- German fund managers tend to prefer German-language tools

### 🇱🇺 LUXEMBOURG (CSSF) — Largest EU Fund Domicile
**Status:** 🟢 Draft published  
**NCA:** CSSF (Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier)

**Transposition Progress:**
- **Draft Bill No. 8628** submitted to Parliament on 3 October 2025
- Amends the Act of 12 July 2013 on AIFMs and UCI Law (17 December 2010)
- No extra burdens beyond directive minimum ("gold-plating")
- Key provisions: expanded AIFM/ManCo services, loan-originating AIF rules, LMT requirements
- Expected to enter into force on 16 April 2026
- Reporting obligations apply from 16 April 2027

**Annex IV Reporting:**
- CSSF has detailed technical guidance (Version 1.7, July 2024)
- XML format must match current ESMA version exactly
- Reporting member state code must be "LU"
- CSSF has its own filing portal

**Market Size:** **~4,000+ fund structures**, ~300+ authorized AIFMs. **Largest fund domicile in Europe.**

**Opportunity:**
- Massive market but VERY competitive — Big 4 (PwC, EY, Deloitte, KPMG) all have strong Luxembourg practices
- Fund-XP, Matterhorn RS, and other specialized vendors already established
- Opportunity is in the **AIFMD 2.0 transition** — new delegation disclosures, loan-originating fund reporting
- ManCos expanding services need updated compliance frameworks

## 📋 ANNEX IV REPORTING: CURRENT STATE & AIFMD 2.0 CHANGES

### Current Regime (AIFMD I)
- **Format:** ESMA XML schema (currently rev 6, updated Nov 2023)
- **Content:** 41-question report covering portfolio, exposures, leverage, liquidity, risk
- **Filing:** To national NCAs via their respective portals
- **Frequency:** Quarterly, semi-annual, or annual based on AUM thresholds
- **Deadline:** Within 30 days of reporting period end

### Filing Methods by Country

| Country | NCA | Portal/Method | Notes |
|---------|-----|---------------|-------|
| 🇩🇪 Germany | BaFin | **MVP-Portal** | XML only, no other format accepted |
| 🇱🇺 Luxembourg | CSSF | CSSF Portal | XML per ESMA, "LU" reporting code, detailed v1.7 guidance |
| 🇮🇪 Ireland | CBI | CBI Online Portal | ESMA standard XML |
| 🇫🇷 France | AMF | **GECO Portal** | ESMA XML + French national specificities |
| 🇪🇸 Spain | CNMV | **CIFRADOC** | ESMA XML |
| 🇮🇹 Italy | CONSOB/BdI | Dual submission | ESMA XML to both regulators |
| 🇳🇱 Netherlands | AFM | AFM Portal | ESMA XML |
| 🇦🇹 Austria | FMA | FMA Portal | ESMA XML |
| 🇲🇹 Malta | MFSA | MFSA Portal | ESMA XML per MFSA guidelines |
| 🇳🇴 Norway | Finanstilsynet | Mandatory XML from June 2026 | Transitioning to mandatory XML |
| 🇬🇧 UK (ref) | FCA | FCA Portal | Own guidance, follows ESMA structure |

### What Changes Under AIFMD 2.0 (Article 24 Revised)

| Area | Current (AIFMD I) | AIFMD 2.0 | Impact |
|------|--------------------|-----------|--------|
| **Scope** | Principal markets/instruments only | **All** markets/instruments/exposures | Massive data expansion |
| **Leverage** | Limited detail | Total leverage per AIF + limits | Enhanced calculation requirements |
| **Marketing** | No jurisdiction disclosure | Must identify Member States | Cross-border transparency |
| **Delegation** | Minimal/unstructured | Detailed structured disclosures (delegate name, domicile, regulatory status, % AUM, staff count, due diligence dates) | New XML sections |
| **Format** | National regulator defined | **EU-standardized, ESMA transmission** | Harmonization |
| **Loan Origination** | Not addressed | Dedicated loan portfolio reporting | New template sections |
| **Transmission** | To NCA only | NCA → **ESMA central database** | Data centralization |

### Timeline for New Reporting
- **April 2026:** Transposition of directive (non-reporting provisions)
- **April 2027:** New Annex IV reporting requirements apply
- **ESMA standardized templates:** Expected to be finalized in 2026-2027
- **RTS on open-ended loan-originating funds:** Not before October 2027

## 🌍 THIRD-COUNTRY IMPLICATIONS

### 🇬🇧 United Kingdom
- UK retains its own AIFMD framework (UK AIFMD) post-Brexit
- FCA has its own Annex IV reporting requirements (follows ESMA structure)
- UK AIFMs marketing into EU must comply with national private placement regimes
- AIFMD II introduces stricter requirements for non-EU managers — UK firms affected
- **Opportunity:** UK managers marketing into EU need cross-border compliance tools

### 🇨🇭 Switzerland
- Swiss managers use national private placement regimes to access EU
- AIFMD II tightens third-country requirements
- Must not be on FATF non-cooperative list or EU non-cooperative tax jurisdiction list
- **Opportunity:** Swiss managers need help understanding new EU requirements
