### 2.3 AIFMD / Annex IV Product — Detailed Analysis

**Source:** [anevis-solutions.com/aifmd-annex-iv/](https://www.anevis-solutions.com/aifmd-annex-iv/)

**Delivery model: MANAGED SERVICE (not self-service SaaS)**

Key claims from their Annex IV page:
- "Complete managed service" — they do the work, not the customer
- Prepare both **AIFM-level reports** and **individual AIF reports** (all types: 24_1, 24_2, 24_2_4, 24_4)
- Generate **ESMA-compliant XML** files
- **Integrated risk calculations** — VaR, DV01, Equity Delta, FX Delta, Currency Delta computed in-platform
- **Dissemination service** — they submit to the NCA (e.g., BaFin MVP-Portal) on the client's behalf
- "Reporting specialists provide personal support"
- "Audit-proof, DORA-compliant IT infrastructure"
- Full historical archive of submissions
- Cross-sells to Marketing Suite (factsheets reuse validated data) and Risk Suite

**What this tells us:**
- anevis sells **high-touch managed service**, not self-service software
- Their USP is "we do everything for you, including risk calc"
- They eliminate the "two-vendor problem" (risk provider + reporting provider)
- They handle NCA submission — client doesn't touch the portal
- This model is **expensive** but **low-effort for the client**

## 5. CAELITH'S DEFENSIBLE ADVANTAGES

### What We Can Do That anevis Can't/Won't

| Advantage | Why Defensible |
|-----------|---------------|
| **1. Self-service model** | anevis built 12 years of process around managed service. Rebuilding as self-service would cannibalize their high-margin model and require completely different UX, support structure, and pricing. They WON'T do this — it would destroy their economics. |
| **2. Transparent low pricing** | anevis's 100+ customer base expects white-glove service. Introducing a €390/mo tier would either cannibalize existing revenue or create a confusing two-tier brand. |
| **3. AIFMD II first-mover content** | anevis blogs ~6 times/year about partnerships and events. They don't do technical regulatory content. Building a content engine is a culture change, not a feature. |
| **4. §35 KAGB + delegation disclosures** | These are Germany-specific / AIFMD II-specific requirements that anevis's broader, multi-product approach deprioritizes. Their dev team is spread across 4 suites + factsheets. |
| **5. Solo-founder speed** | When ESMA publishes the new schema, Caelith can ship in 2-4 weeks. anevis has product management, QA, client coordination, multi-suite dependencies — 2-4 months minimum. |
| **6. Fund admin API play** | anevis offers managed service TO fund admins. Caelith can offer API infrastructure FOR fund admins to self-serve, which scales differently. |

| "Advantage" | Why Not Defensible |

## 6. COMPETITIVE RESPONSE SCENARIOS

### Scenario A: anevis ships AIFMD II update (likely Q3-Q4 2026)

**Probability:** 90%  
**Timeline:** Within 3-6 months of ESMA publishing final schema  
**Impact on Caelith:** Eliminates "first mover" narrative

**Caelith response:**
- Must have paying customers BEFORE anevis ships — first-mover advantage only works if you actually moved first
- Shift narrative from "we have AIFMD 2.0" to "we're purpose-built for it" — our whole product is AIFMD II, theirs is an update to a factsheet platform
- Emphasize: self-service vs. managed service, transparent pricing, instant onboarding

### Scenario B: anevis launches a "light" tier for small KVGs

**Probability:** 20%  
**Timeline:** 6-12 months if triggered by market demand  
**Impact on Caelith:** Direct competition on price

**Caelith response:**
- They'd cannibalize their own margins — unlikely unless Caelith is visibly stealing customers
- If they do: compete on UX, speed, modern tech, AIFMD-II-native architecture
- Their "light" tier would be a stripped-down version of managed service, not truly self-service

### Scenario C: ARKK or Matterhorn enters German market

**Probability:** 30%  
**Timeline:** 12-18 months  
**Impact on Caelith:** Another competitor for mid-market

**Caelith response:**
- German-language advantage is real against UK/LU competitors
- BaFin MVP-Portal specific expertise (they'd need to build)
- §35 KAGB support they can't easily replicate

### Scenario D: ESMA delays schema publication to 2027

**Probability:** 30%  
**Timeline:** Announcement in H2 2026  
**Impact on Caelith:** Urgency evaporates, sales cycle extends

**Caelith response:**
- Diversify product: compliance monitoring, delegation disclosures, LMT wizards don't depend on ESMA schema
- Sell readiness/preparation, not just filing
- This is the most dangerous scenario — requires runway to survive

### Scenario E: Big 4 firm partners with or acquires anevis

**Probability:** 10-15%  
**Timeline:** Anytime  
**Impact on Caelith:** anevis gets enterprise distribution

**Caelith response:**
- Actually helpful — acquisition would likely push anevis further upmarket, opening mid/small segment more
- Big 4 acquisitions often kill product agility

## 7. SALES BATTLECARDS

### 7.1 "We already use anevis"

**Prospect context:** Mid/large KVG using anevis for factsheets + Annex IV

**Response framework:**

> "anevis is a great platform for fund factsheets and broad regulatory reporting — we respect what they've built. The question is whether their generalist approach is the right fit for the **specific AIFMD II compliance challenges** hitting in April 2027. Three things to consider:
>
> 1. **Are they self-service?** When your compliance team needs to run a quick Annex IV check at 4pm on deadline day, can they do it themselves? Or do they need to email anevis and wait?
>
> 2. **AIFMD II specifics:** Have they shown you their updated delegation disclosure templates? Their loan origination reporting module? Their §35 KAGB integration? These are new requirements that need purpose-built solutions.
>
> 3. **Cost of the bundle you don't need:** You're likely paying for their Marketing Suite + Regulatory Suite + Risk Suite integration. If you only need AIFMD compliance, you're subsidizing factsheet infrastructure.
>
> We're not suggesting you replace anevis for factsheets. But for AIFMD II compliance specifically, a purpose-built tool at a fraction of the cost might make more sense."

**Key objections and responses:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|---------|
| "anevis does our risk calculations" | "That's valuable for Annex IV. But AIFMD II adds delegation disclosures, LMT documentation, and loan origination reporting — areas where risk calculations don't help. We handle those." |
| "We don't want another vendor" | "We understand vendor fatigue. That's why we're self-service — no managed service overhead, no specialist coordination. Your team runs it directly." |
| "anevis will update for AIFMD II" | "They will — eventually. The question is when. We're live today with AIFMD II readiness tools. When ESMA publishes the schema, we'll ship within weeks, not months." |

| **Content / thought leadership** | ❌ Weak blog, no regulatory content | ✅ Planned | **Caelith** (if executed) |

**Bottom line:** anevis wins on breadth, trust, and established customer base. Caelith wins on focus, speed, accessibility, and AIFMD II specificity. This is a classic **incumbent broad suite vs. focused disruptor** dynamic. Caelith shouldn't try to beat anevis at their own game (managed service for enterprises). Instead, own the segments anevis doesn't serve well: small KVGs, self-service buyers, and the AIFMD II transition specifically.
