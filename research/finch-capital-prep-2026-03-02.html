Why Agents Are the Moat — Not the Feature

The dashboard is replicable in 10 weeks (we proved it). The agent layer is not. Agents require: deep regulatory domain knowledge to handle edge cases; integration with multiple NCA portals (each with different auth, formats, error handling); XSD validation logic that changes with every ESMA schema release; a cryptographic audit trail that satisfies DORA; and machine learning to handle portal UI drift without breaking. This is 12–18 months of regulatory-domain engineering that you can't outsource to a generic AI vendor.

Sphinx raised $7.1M. Kobalt raised $12.7M. Both US-only. Neither has ESMA XSD, BaFin portal automation, or EU fund manager domain knowledge. The agent layer is where vertical depth beats horizontal scale.

Hard Questions — Best Answers

Q: "Most KVGs outsource compliance to their fund admin for free. Your real market is tiny."
This is the best objection you'll face. Welcome it.

"You're right — and that makes the business better. If KVGs outsource to fund admins, we don't need 3,500 sales conversations. We need 50. One fund admin like HANSAINVEST manages hundreds of KVGs. One deal = €50-200K ARR. We sell infrastructure to the fund admin — they use our API for all the funds they manage. The TAM doesn't shrink; the sales cycle gets dramatically more capital-efficient."
Q: "You're pre-revenue with no customers. Why should we care?"
"The April 16 deadline creates a forcing function that didn't exist 6 months ago. We have a working product with validated XML output — not a slide deck. The filing window opened yesterday. We're not selling a future roadmap; we're selling something compliance managers need right now."
Q: "anevis has 100+ clients and a decade of experience. How do you compete?"
"We're not competing with anevis — we're replacing the model. anevis is a managed service: €50-80K/year, humans assigned to each client, 3-4 week turnaround. We're self-service: €990/mo, instant, automated, 24/7. What takes anevis a month takes us seconds. And the agentic shift they're not positioned for — Sphinx raised $7.1M for the same thesis in US fintech. anevis can't compete with autonomous agents any more than travel agents could compete with Booking.com."
Q: "It's just you. Solo founder, no co-founder. What's the bus factor?"
"Valid — and it's why the first hire is the first line item in the budget. But the product runs without me: CI pipeline, 231 automated tests, architecture docs, security audit, ops runbook. Two weeks offline and the product keeps serving customers. What stops is iteration speed — that's exactly what hire #1 fixes. The solo phase was a feature: one vision, 10 weeks, live product."
Q: "How big is this market really?"
"Germany alone: 704 BaFin-registered KVGs × €24K ACV = €16.8M ceiling. Realistic Y1: 35 customers at 5% capture = €836K ARR. The real scale is fund admins — Germany has roughly 15–25 significant fund admins. The top 5 (Universal Investment, HANSAINVEST, Caceis, DWS, BNP Paribas Securities) each serve 50–150 KVGs. One deal with HANSAINVEST covers their entire client base — that's potentially €100–200K ARR from a single contract. That's the capital efficiency of selling infrastructure, not seats. EU-wide: 3,500+ AIFMs, $18B global RegTech market, Europe is ~35% = ~€6B. Asset management = 15-20% of that = €1-2B SAM. The wedge is Germany. The vision is EU infrastructure."

⚠️ Note: the Annex IV filing unit is the AIF (the fund), not the KVG (the manager). One KVG managing 3 funds = 3 filings = 3× the billable volume. Germany's real addressable volume is ~1,500–2,000 annual filings, not 704.
Q: "There are existing tools for this — Funds-Axis, SSCTech, DataTracks. What do you do that they don't?"
Acknowledge they exist — it actually validates the market.

"Yes — and their existence proves this is a real problem that people pay for. The distinction: Funds-Axis and SSCTech are enterprise platforms built for fund administrators and large custodians — minimum contracts, implementation projects, UK/Ireland-centric, no self-service. A German KVG with 2 funds and a 2-person compliance team can't buy Funds-Axis. They're left with Excel and consultants.

We're the self-service layer that doesn't exist: log in, upload your data, get XSD-validated XML in minutes, €990/month. And beyond that — these platforms have zero agent layer. No BaFin portal automation, no overnight autonomous filing. That's the 2-3 year moat they can't replicate without rebuilding their architecture."
Q: "Why wouldn't Bloomberg or Big 4 just build this?"
"Big 4 won't — it would cannibalize advisory revenue. Bloomberg builds horizontal tools. AIFMD Annex IV requires deep understanding of 8+ national regulators, different filing portals, languages, and requirements — plus ongoing schema changes. We're vertical and obsessed with one problem. That's our advantage over any horizontal player."
Q: "Open-core — why give anything away for free?"
"We open-source the XML serializer — the plumbing. We keep the intelligence, automation, and infrastructure proprietary. The library is a developer acquisition channel: fund admin engineers integrate it, discover Caelith, and realize they need the 95% of value above it. Same model as Elastic ($2B+ revenue), HashiCorp ($6B acquisition), MongoDB ($30B+ market cap). Open the interface layer, monetize the managed service."
Q: "Your €400K breakdown has €115K buffer. That's 29%."
"Deliberate. We're in regulated financial services — unexpected costs are the rule. ISO 27001 scope can vary by €20K. Legal for DORA compliance isn't predictable. A fund admin integration might need a custom sprint. I'd rather tell you upfront than come back in 12 months saying we ran out. Capital efficiency matters, but not dying mid-execution matters more."
Q: "March 2027 — €400K spent, zero customers. What happens?"
"We have kill criteria at every stage. If <3 of 10 discovery calls show interest by Week 4 → pivot channel to fund admins. If zero pilots convert by Month 6 → pivot to standalone API product — the regulatory data engine has value beyond AIFMD. If zero revenue after 12 months and 50+ conversations → shut down and return remaining capital. I'd rather kill a thesis early than drag it on hope."
Q: "Have you actually tested filing to BaFin's portal?"
"I've mapped the full BaFin MVP Portal workflow — standard form, no CAPTCHA, no complex auth, XML upload. Our XML validates against ESMA's official XSD schema — that's the hard part. Portal automation is 2-3 weeks of engineering. I won't claim a live submission I haven't done. What I can show is the XML that would be submitted — and it passes schema validation."

Q1 — Compliance as competitive moat (use as closer)

Where the first KVGs on automated Annex IV filing start using speed-to-market as an LP pitch — 'we can launch a new fund structure and be fully compliant in weeks, not months'?"

Why it works: Studies her portfolio. Reframes compliance from cost centre to competitive weapon — a thesis shift that makes her think. Invites her to extend or disagree, which is what good VC conversations feel like.
Q2 — AMLR sequencing (use early — shows you're looking around the corner)
"Fourthline exists because fintechs hit a KYC wall they weren't built for. AMLR 2027 extends full AML obligations to fund managers for the first time — obligations banks have had for decades. Do you see the same infrastructure deficit forming? Because Caelith sits exactly at that intersection: Annex IV is the wedge, AMLR is the wave behind it."

Why it works: Shows you've looked past the current pitch to the next one. Most pre-seed founders pitch the problem in front of them — you're pitching the sequence. That's how a Series A story gets built. This gets you into the tracker.
Q3 — EU regulatory fragmentation as moat or ceiling (use early — genuinely open-ended)
"Here's something I go back and forth on and I'd genuinely love your take: EU regulatory fragmentation — BaFin doing things differently from CSSF from AMF — is usually pitched as a problem. I think of it as a 3-5 year defensibility window before any harmonisation happens. A US player can't just enter Germany with a generic compliance tool. Has that fragmentation dynamic played out as a moat in any of your portfolio companies, or does it eventually become a ceiling?"

Why it works: Genuinely open — you don't know her answer. Positions you as someone who wrestles with hard strategic questions. Forces her to think about Caelith's defensibility specifically. Rare for a pre-seed founder to bring this level of nuance.
Q4 — Fund admin buy vs. build inflection (use as closer — pre-empts their obvious pushback)
"The obvious path is selling directly to 3,500 KVGs. The capital-efficient path is selling to 50 fund admins who each manage 20-30 funds. I keep coming back to one question: fund admins build when it's core product, they buy when it's overhead. Annex IV is overhead for them. Have you seen that buy-vs-build inflection tip in any of your portfolio companies — what unlocked it?"

Why it works: Shows you've already stress-tested the pivot she or her partners might suggest. You're not defending one go-to-market — you're thinking out loud. Signals intellectual honesty and strategic maturity. Puts her in the role of advisor, which people enjoy.
