## What is AIFMD II? {#what-is-aifmd-ii}

AIFMD II — formally **Directive (EU) 2024/927** — is the comprehensive revision of the original Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (AIFMD, Directive 2011/61/EU). Published in the Official Journal of the European Union on March 26, 2024, it represents the most significant overhaul of EU alternative investment fund regulation in over a decade.

The original AIFMD, enacted in 2011 and transposed by 2013, established the first pan-European framework for regulating managers of alternative investment funds (AIFs) — including hedge funds, private equity funds, real estate funds, and other non-UCITS vehicles. While AIFMD I created a functioning passport system and harmonized core rules around authorization, depositary requirements, and transparency, a decade of practical experience exposed gaps.

AIFMD II addresses those gaps head-on. It simultaneously amends both the AIFMD and the UCITS Directive (2009/65/EC), bringing unified rules across the managed fund spectrum. The directive was adopted by the European Parliament on February 7, 2024, and by the Council on March 26, 2024.

**The enforcement deadline is April 16, 2026.** By that date, all EU Member States must transpose the directive into national law, and fund managers must comply with the new requirements.

AIFMD II is not a revolution — it's an evolution. The core architecture of AIFMD I remains intact: the authorization regime, the passport, the depositary framework. What changes is the depth and specificity of requirements in areas where regulators identified weaknesses, particularly around delegation, liquidity management, loan origination, and regulatory reporting.

For alternative investment fund managers (AIFMs), the practical impact ranges from significant operational changes (new liquidity management tools, overhauled Annex IV reporting) to more nuanced adjustments (enhanced disclosure requirements, refined delegation rules). For UCITS management companies, certain AIFMD II provisions now apply for the first time, creating new obligations that didn't previously exist.

## Key Changes from AIFMD I {#key-changes}

AIFMD II introduces changes across seven major areas. Here's what each means in practice.

### 1. Enhanced Delegation & Substance Requirements

The delegation regime was one of the most politically contentious aspects of AIFMD II's development. Post-Brexit concerns about "letterbox entities" — AIFMs authorized in the EU that delegate virtually all portfolio management to third-country firms — drove significant pressure for reform.

**What's new:**

- **Substance requirements formalized under Article 20**: AIFMs must employ at least two senior managers who are EU residents and who are engaged full-time, or employ a sufficient number of persons with the necessary knowledge and expertise to effectively oversee delegated functions.
- **Enhanced notification to NCAs**: AIFMs must notify their home NCA before establishing or materially amending any delegation arrangement. The notification must include details on the delegate, the scope of delegation, and the supervisory arrangements in place.
- **Annual reporting on delegation**: AIFMs must report annually to their NCA on the substance and nature of their delegation arrangements, including the human and technical resources retained.
- **ESMA peer reviews**: ESMA will conduct periodic peer reviews of NCA supervisory practices on delegation, with public findings.

The directive stops short of imposing quantitative caps on delegation (as some MEPs had pushed for), but the enhanced transparency and reporting requirements effectively force AIFMs to demonstrate genuine substance.

**Key articles**: Article 20 (Delegation), Article 24 (Reporting obligations)

### 2. Liquidity Management Tools — Now Mandatory

Perhaps the single most impactful operational change. Under AIFMD I, liquidity management tools (LMTs) were available but not required. AIFMD II makes them mandatory.

**What's new:**

- **Mandatory selection under Article 16(2b)**: Every open-ended AIF must select at least two liquidity management tools from a harmonized EU-wide list, one of which must be either the suspension of redemptions or redemption gates.
- **The harmonized toolkit** includes:
  - Suspension of redemptions
  - Redemption gates
  - Extension of notice periods
  - Redemption fees
  - Swing pricing
  - Anti-dilution levies
  - Redemptions in kind
  - Side pockets
- **Activation conditions**: AIFMs must define in advance the circumstances under which each tool will be activated and document these in the fund's constitutional documents.
- **NCA notification**: AIFMs must notify their NCA when activating suspension of redemptions or redemption gates for any fund marketed cross-border.
- **Extends to UCITS**: Equivalent LMT requirements now apply to UCITS management companies under the parallel amendments to Directive 2009/65/EC.

This is a genuine operational lift. Fund managers need to review every open-ended AIF's documentation, select appropriate tools, define activation procedures, and update offering documents — all before April 16, 2026.

**Key articles**: Article 16(2a)-(2d) AIFMD, Article 18a UCITS Directive

### 3. Loan Origination Rules — An Entirely New Chapter

AIFMD II introduces the first EU-wide framework for loan-originating AIFs. Previously, loan origination by funds was regulated inconsistently (or not at all) across Member States.

**What's new:**

- **New Article 15a-15e**: A dedicated regulatory framework for AIFs that originate loans as part of their investment strategy.
- **Retention requirements**: Loan-originating AIFs must retain at least 5% of the notional value of loans they originate and subsequently sell or securitize — for the life of the loan.
- **Concentration limits**: No single borrower may account for more than 20% of a loan-originating AIF's capital (with limited exceptions for lending to financial institutions or public entities).
- **Leverage cap**: Closed-ended loan-originating AIFs are subject to a 300% leverage limit; open-ended loan-originating AIFs face a 175% limit.
- **Prohibition on lending to related parties**: AIFs cannot originate loans to their AIFM, the AIFM's staff, the depositary, or the AIFM's delegates.
- **Effective credit risk policies**: AIFMs must implement, maintain, and apply documented credit risk assessment policies and procedures.

**Transitional provision**: Existing loan-originating AIFs established before April 16, 2026 benefit from a transitional period — they need not comply with the concentration and leverage limits for existing loans originated before that date, though new originations must comply immediately.

**Key articles**: Articles 15a-15e

### 4. Annex IV Reporting — Expanded and Overhauled

Annex IV reporting — the periodic regulatory return that AIFMs file with their national competent authority (NCA) — undergoes its most significant revision since AIFMD I's inception. This is covered in detail in the [dedicated section below](#annex-iv-reporting).

**Headlines:**

- Substantially expanded data fields (from approximately 300 to over 500 reportable items)
- New reporting on liquidity management tools, loan origination activity, and ESG/sustainability metrics
- Revised filing frequencies for smaller managers
- Adoption of the new ESMA XML schema (XSD Rev 6 v1.2)
- Enhanced leverage reporting with more granular breakdowns
- New fields for delegation arrangements

**Key articles**: Article 24 AIFMD, Annex IV (as amended), ESMA RTS on reporting

### 5. Leverage Reporting Enhancements

AIFMD II significantly refines the leverage reporting framework, addressing criticisms that the original commitment and gross methods were too blunt to capture systemic risk effectively.

**What's new:**

- **Revised leverage calculation methodologies**: ESMA has developed Regulatory Technical Standards (RTS) refining both the gross and commitment methods, with clearer netting and hedging rules.
- **Additional leverage metrics**: AIFMs employing leverage on a substantial basis must report additional risk metrics, including sensitivity-based measures for certain asset classes.
- **NCA leverage limits**: Article 25 is amended to give NCAs an explicit, harmonized framework for imposing leverage limits on individual AIFs where financial stability risks are identified. ESMA coordinates and can issue opinions on proposed limits.
- **Reverse stress testing**: AIFMs of leveraged AIFs must conduct regular reverse stress tests and report results to the NCA upon request.

**Key articles**: Article 25 (Leverage limits), Article 24(4) (Leverage reporting), ESMA RTS on leverage calculation

### 6. Investor Disclosure Improvements

AIFMD II enhances the pre-contractual and ongoing disclosure requirements under Article 23, with a focus on fee transparency and alignment of interest.

**What's new:**

- **Fee and cost disclosure**: AIFMs must disclose all fees, charges, and expenses borne by the AIF, directly or indirectly — including a detailed breakdown of management fees, performance fees, depositary fees, and transaction costs. Historical fee data must be included.
- **Portfolio composition disclosure**: More granular disclosure of the AIF's portfolio composition, including illiquid asset percentages.
- **Delegation disclosure**: Where portfolio or risk management is delegated, the AIFM must disclose the identity of the delegate, the jurisdiction, and the scope of delegation.
- **Open-ended fund disclosures**: For open-ended AIFs, enhanced disclosure of the liquidity management tools available and the conditions under which they may be activated.

**Key articles**: Article 23 (Disclosure to investors)

### 7. Third-Country AIFM Rules

AIFMD II refines the framework for third-country (non-EU) AIFMs accessing EU markets, though the most significant change — activation of the third-country passport under Articles 35-37 — remains deferred.

**What's new:**

- **Enhanced national private placement regime (NPPR) requirements**: Third-country AIFMs marketing under NPPR must provide more extensive information to NCAs, including on delegation arrangements and substance.
- **ESMA coordination**: ESMA gains a coordination role in monitoring NPPR usage, with NCAs required to report annually on third-country AIFM marketing activity.
- **Supervisory cooperation agreements**: Updated and expanded requirements for cooperation agreements between NCAs and third-country supervisors.
- **No activation of third-country passport**: The full third-country passport (Articles 35-37 of AIFMD I) remains inactive. Third-country managers continue to rely on NPPRs, which vary by Member State.

**Key articles**: Articles 35-37 (Third-country provisions), Article 42 (NPPR)

### Transposition Status by Country (as of February 2026)

**Luxembourg** 🟢 — First mover. Luxembourg transposed AIFMD II via the Law of November 21, 2025, amending the Laws of February 13, 2007 (SIFs), December 17, 2010 (UCIs), and July 12, 2013 (AIFMs). The CSSF published its implementing circular in December 2025, providing detailed guidance on the new liquidity management tool requirements and loan origination framework. [More on CSSF requirements →](/nca/cssf)

**Germany** 🟡 — Draft legislation in progress. The German transposition is embedded in the broader **Fondsmarktstärkungsgesetz** (Fund Market Strengthening Act), which amends the Kapitalanlagegesetzbuch (KAGB). The draft was published in late 2025 and is expected to be enacted by the Bundestag in Q1 2026. BaFin has issued preliminary guidance but the final regulatory framework depends on the Fondsmarktstärkungsgesetz's passage. [More on BaFin requirements →](/nca/bafin)

**France** 🟡 — Draft ordinance (ordonnance) circulated in late 2025 for consultation. The Autorité des marchés financiers (AMF) is expected to publish updated General Regulations (Règlement général) and specific instructions in Q1 2026. France is on track for transposition by the deadline, though implementing details remain pending.

**Ireland** 🟡 — The Central Bank of Ireland (CBI) has issued a consultation paper on the transposition of AIFMD II, with a focus on the liquidity management tool requirements and Annex IV reporting. Final rules expected Q1 2026. Ireland's implementation is particularly significant given Dublin's role as a major AIF domicile.

**Spain** 🔴 — Delayed. The Comisión Nacional del Mercado de Valores (CNMV) has not yet published draft implementing legislation. Spain is expected to miss the April 16, 2026 deadline, following a pattern of late transposition seen with AIFMD I and other EU financial services directives.

**Italy** 🟡 — Draft legislation in progress. Consob and the Banca d'Italia are coordinating on implementation.

**Netherlands** 🟡 — The Autoriteit Financiële Markten (AFM) has published guidance; draft legislation expected Q1 2026.

**United Kingdom** 🔵 — **Not applicable.** Post-Brexit, the UK is not subject to AIFMD II. The FCA continues to operate under the UK's domestic AIFMD regime (as onshored via the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018). However, UK managers marketing into the EU via NPPRs must comply with the enhanced NPPR requirements under AIFMD II. The FCA has signaled it may undertake its own review of the UK AIFM regime, but no concrete proposals have emerged.

### Transitional Provisions

AIFMD II includes several transitional provisions:

- **Existing loan-originating AIFs**: AIFs that originated loans before April 16, 2026 are not required to comply with the new concentration limits (Article 15a(3)) and leverage caps (Article 15a(4)) for loans originated before that date. New originations must comply immediately.
- **Annex IV reporting**: The first filings under the new ESMA reporting schema are expected for reporting periods beginning on or after April 16, 2026. NCAs may provide additional transitional guidance on filing timelines.
- **Liquidity management tools**: AIFMs must have selected and documented their LMTs, and updated fund documentation, by April 16, 2026.

## Annex IV Reporting Under AIFMD II {#annex-iv-reporting}

For most AIFMs, the changes to [Annex IV reporting](/glossary/annex-iv) represent the single largest operational burden. The reporting framework — the periodic return filed with national competent authorities — has been fundamentally expanded.

### What's Changing in the Schema

The European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) has developed a revised XML reporting schema — **XSD Revision 6, Version 1.2** — that reflects the expanded data requirements. Key changes include:

**New data categories:**
- **Liquidity management tools**: AIFMs must report which LMTs are available for each fund, whether they have been activated during the reporting period, and the circumstances of activation.
- **Loan origination data**: Entirely new reporting section for loan-originating AIFs covering loan portfolio composition, borrower concentration, retention compliance, and credit risk metrics.
- **ESG and sustainability fields**: New fields capturing sustainable investment objectives, SFDR classification (Article 8 or 9), principal adverse impact indicators, and taxonomy alignment percentages.
- **Delegation reporting**: Granular reporting on delegation arrangements, including the identity and jurisdiction of delegates, the functions delegated, and human resource allocation.

**Expanded existing categories:**
- **Leverage reporting**: Revised calculation methodology fields, additional gross/commitment breakdowns, and new sensitivity-based metrics for derivatives-heavy portfolios.
- **Liquidity profile**: More granular liquidity bucketing, with separate reporting for investor-side liquidity (redemption terms) and portfolio-side liquidity (time to liquidation).
- **Counterparty exposure**: Expanded counterparty fields including LEI requirements, central clearing vs. bilateral OTC breakdowns, and margin/collateral details.
- **Investment strategy detail**: Finer granularity on sub-strategies, particularly for credit and real estate funds.

### XSD Rev 6 v1.2 — Technical Details

The new schema introduces:

- Approximately **200+ new XML elements** compared to the current schema (XSD Rev 5)
- New complex types for loan origination (`LoanOriginationDataType`), LMT activation (`LMTActivationType`), and ESG metrics (`SustainabilityMetricsType`)
- Revised enumeration lists for investment strategies, instrument types, and counterparty classifications
- **LEI validation**: Mandatory Legal Entity Identifier fields for counterparties, delegates, and service providers — with XSD-level format validation
- UTF-8 encoding requirement (unchanged)
- Maximum file size increased to 50MB (from 20MB) to accommodate the expanded data

### Filing Frequencies by AUM Threshold

| AIFM Category | AIF Category | Filing Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| AIFM managing > €5bn AUM | All managed AIFs | **Quarterly** |
| AIFM managing > €1.5bn AUM | AIFs with AuM > €500m | **Quarterly** |
| AIFM managing > €1.5bn AUM | AIFs with AuM ≤ €500m | **Semi-annually** |
| AIFM managing ≤ €1.5bn AUM | All managed AIFs | **Semi-annually** |
| AIFM managing < €500m (registered) | All managed AIFs | **Annually** |
| Any AIFM | Loan-originating AIFs (any size) | **Quarterly** |

**Note**: The quarterly filing requirement for all loan-originating AIFs regardless of AUM is a new addition under AIFMD II, reflecting regulatory concern about this asset class.

### NCA-Specific Requirements

While ESMA sets the minimum framework, individual NCAs retain the ability to impose additional reporting requirements. Key NCA-specific considerations:

- **BaFin (Germany)**: Expected to require additional national data fields related to the KAGB amendments. BaFin's electronic filing system (MVP Portal) is being updated to accept the new schema. [BaFin requirements →](/nca/bafin)
- **CSSF (Luxembourg)**: Has published detailed filing instructions for the new schema. Luxembourg adds fields for RAIF-specific reporting. Filing via the CSSF's eDesk platform. [CSSF requirements →](/nca/cssf)
- **AMF (France)**: Expected to require French-language descriptions for certain narrative fields. Filing via the GECO platform.
- **CBI (Ireland)**: ONR (Online Reporting) system being updated. CBI expected to align closely with ESMA minimum requirements.
- **FCA (UK)**: Continues to require AIFMD Annex IV reporting under the UK's domestic regime, but using the current (pre-AIFMD II) schema. UK AIFMs marketing into the EU must file under the new schema with the relevant EU NCA.

### Common Pitfalls in Annex IV Filing

Based on years of working with fund managers on regulatory reporting, these are the most common errors:

1. **Incorrect AUM calculation methodology** — Using NAV rather than the Article 2 AUM definition (which includes leverage)
2. **Missing LEIs** — The new schema requires LEIs for counterparties; many managers track counterparties by name only
3. **Liquidity bucketing mismatches** — Investor-side and portfolio-side liquidity profiles using inconsistent time horizons
4. **FX exposure double-counting** — Reporting hedged and unhedged positions incorrectly in the gross leverage calculation
5. **Stale data** — Filing based on month-end data when the reporting date falls mid-quarter

## Impact by Jurisdiction {#jurisdictions}

### Germany

Germany's transposition of AIFMD II is proceeding via the **Fondsmarktstärkungsgesetz** (Fund Market Strengthening Act), a broader legislative package that also addresses other priorities in the German fund industry.

**Key aspects of the German implementation:**

- **KAGB amendments**: The Kapitalanlagegesetzbuch (KAGB) — Germany's primary fund legislation — is being amended extensively to incorporate AIFMD II requirements. Changes affect Sections 26 (liquidity management), 29 (delegation), 285-292 (special rules for open-ended funds), and numerous other provisions.
- **BaFin supervisory approach**: BaFin has signaled it will take a proportionate approach to enforcement in the first year post-implementation, while expecting full compliance from Day 1 on the core requirements (LMTs, Annex IV reporting).
- **Loan origination**: Germany previously had relatively permissive rules on loan origination by funds under the KAGB. The new AIFMD II framework creates a more structured regime that may require existing loan funds to adjust their structures.
- **Semi-professional investors**: Germany's unique "semi-professional investor" category (qualified private investors with minimum €200,000 commitment) is preserved, with AIFMD II's investor disclosure requirements applied proportionately.

[Detailed BaFin compliance guide →](/nca/bafin)

### Luxembourg

Luxembourg was the **first EU Member State to transpose AIFMD II**, with the implementing law enacted on November 21, 2025. This is consistent with Luxembourg's strategic priority as Europe's leading fund domicile.

**Key aspects of the Luxembourg implementation:**

- **CSSF Circular 25/xxx**: The CSSF published detailed implementing guidance in December 2025, covering practical aspects of LMT selection, loan origination rules, and the enhanced Annex IV reporting framework.
- **Impact on RAIF structures**: Reserved Alternative Investment Funds (RAIFs) — which are not directly regulated by the CSSF but managed by authorized AIFMs — are indirectly affected through the AIFM-level requirements.
- **Cross-border implications**: Given that Luxembourg-domiciled AIFs are marketed across Europe, the early transposition provides certainty for managers preparing for the April 2026 deadline.
- **SIF and SICAR amendments**: Specific amendments to the SIF Law (2007) and SICAR Law (2004) align these vehicles with AIFMD II.

[Detailed CSSF compliance guide →](/nca/cssf)

### France

France is transposing AIFMD II via **ordonnance** (delegated legislation), with the AMF responsible for detailed implementing rules in its General Regulations (Règlement général de l'AMF).

**Key aspects:**

- **Draft ordonnance**: Circulated in late 2025 for industry consultation. Broadly faithful to the directive text without significant gold-plating.
- **AMF focus areas**: The AMF has indicated particular focus on the delegation requirements (given France's role as a management hub) and the loan origination framework.
- **Professional fund structures**: Impact on Fonds Professionels Spécialisés (FPS) and Fonds Professionels de Capital Investissement (FPCI) structures.

### Ireland

Ireland's transposition is significant given Dublin's role as a leading AIF service center, particularly for hedge funds and private equity feeder structures.

**Key aspects:**

- **CBI Consultation Paper**: Published mid-2025, addressing the practical implementation of LMT requirements and enhanced reporting.
- **Section 110 companies**: Irish Section 110 qualifying investor AIFs are affected through AIFM-level requirements.
- **Industry preparedness**: The Irish Funds industry body has been active in preparing members, with multiple guidance documents and working groups.

### United Kingdom

The UK is **not subject to AIFMD II**. Post-Brexit, the UK's domestic AIFM regime — based on the onshored AIFMD I — remains in force. However:

- **UK managers marketing into the EU**: Must comply with AIFMD II's enhanced NPPR requirements when marketing to EU investors.
- **FCA review**: The FCA has indicated it may review the UK AIFM regime in due course, potentially incorporating elements of AIFMD II that are deemed appropriate for the UK market. No formal proposals have been published.
- **Divergence risk**: As the EU and UK regimes increasingly diverge, UK-based managers with EU distribution strategies face dual compliance obligations.
- **Annex IV implications**: UK managers marketing under NPPR must file Annex IV reports with the relevant EU NCA under the new AIFMD II schema, while continuing to file UK domestic reports with the FCA under the old schema.

## Compliance Checklist {#compliance-checklist}

A practical, step-by-step roadmap for AIFMs preparing for April 16, 2026.

### Phase 1: Assessment (Now)

1. **Conduct a gap analysis** — Map your current policies, procedures, and documentation against AIFMD II requirements. Identify where you are non-compliant.
2. **Assess your delegation arrangements** — Review all delegation agreements. Do you meet the new substance requirements? Can you evidence senior management time allocation and oversight?
3. **Inventory your AIFs** — Classify each fund: open-ended vs. closed-ended, loan-originating vs. non-loan-originating, leveraged vs. unleveraged. Each classification triggers different requirements.
4. **Check your NCA's transposition status** — Identify which national law applies and any NCA-specific requirements beyond the directive minimum.
5. **Identify your Annex IV reporting gaps** — Do you have the data to populate the new schema fields? LEIs for counterparties? ESG classifications? Loan origination metrics?

### Phase 2: Implementation (Q1 2026)

6. **Select liquidity management tools** — For each open-ended AIF, select at least two LMTs from the harmonized list (one must be redemption suspension or gates). Document activation criteria.
7. **Update fund documentation** — Prospectuses, offering memoranda, and partnership agreements must reflect the new LMT provisions, enhanced fee disclosure, and delegation information.
8. **Implement loan origination compliance** — If applicable: establish retention tracking, concentration limit monitoring, and leverage cap calculation processes.
9. **Upgrade your reporting infrastructure** — Ensure your reporting system can generate Annex IV filings compliant with XSD Rev 6 v1.2. Test file generation and validation.
10. **Train your team** — Compliance officers, risk managers, and portfolio managers all need to understand the new requirements that affect their functions.

### Phase 3: Validation (March–April 2026)

11. **Run a test Annex IV filing** — Generate a complete filing under the new schema and validate it against the XSD. Identify and fix data gaps.
12. **Review all investor disclosures** — Ensure pre-contractual documents meet the enhanced Article 23 requirements. Check fee disclosure completeness.
13. **Document your LMT procedures** — Create or update your LMT activation playbook with clear escalation procedures, board notification templates, and NCA reporting workflows.
14. **Brief your board** — Governing bodies must be aware of the new requirements, particularly around LMT activation authority and enhanced delegation oversight.
15. **Engage with your NCA** — File any required notifications (new delegation arrangements, LMT selections) ahead of the deadline.

## FAQ {#faq}

### When is the AIFMD II deadline?

The AIFMD II transposition and compliance deadline is **April 16, 2026**. By this date, all EU Member States must have transposed Directive (EU) 2024/927 into national law, and all authorized AIFMs (and UCITS management companies, where applicable) must comply with the new requirements. There is no general grace period, although specific transitional provisions apply to existing loan-originating AIFs.

### What are the main changes in AIFMD II?

The seven most significant changes are: (1) mandatory liquidity management tools for open-ended AIFs, (2) a new loan origination framework with retention, concentration, and leverage rules, (3) substantially expanded Annex IV regulatory reporting, (4) enhanced delegation and substance requirements, (5) improved investor disclosure on fees and costs, (6) refined leverage reporting and NCA leverage limit powers, and (7) updated third-country AIFM marketing requirements.

### Does AIFMD II apply to UCITS?

Yes, partially. Directive (EU) 2024/927 simultaneously amends both the AIFMD and the UCITS Directive (2009/65/EC). The key UCITS-relevant changes include mandatory liquidity management tools for UCITS funds, enhanced delegation requirements for UCITS management companies, and improved investor disclosure. The loan origination rules and Annex IV reporting changes are AIFMD-specific and do not apply to UCITS.

### What is Annex IV reporting?

[Annex IV](/glossary/annex-iv) is the periodic regulatory report that Alternative Investment Fund Managers (AIFMs) must file with their national competent authority (NCA) under the AIFMD. It contains detailed information about each managed AIF, including its investment strategy, instruments traded, risk profile, leverage, liquidity, counterparty exposure, and (under AIFMD II) loan origination activity, LMT usage, and ESG metrics. The report is filed in XML format according to ESMA's technical schema.

### How do I generate Annex IV XML?

Annex IV XML files must conform to ESMA's XSD schema (currently Rev 6 v1.2 for AIFMD II). You can generate compliant XML by: (1) using a dedicated reporting platform like [Caelith](/readiness-check) that automates XML generation and validation, (2) building in-house using ESMA's published XSD schema and technical documentation, or (3) engaging a third-party regulatory reporting service. Given the complexity of the new schema (500+ data fields, strict validation rules), most managers opt for an automated solution.

### Which NCAs have transposed AIFMD II?

As of February 2026, **Luxembourg** has fully transposed AIFMD II (Law of November 21, 2025). **Germany**, **France**, **Ireland**, **Italy**, and the **Netherlands** have draft legislation in progress and are expected to meet the April 16, 2026 deadline. **Spain** is expected to miss the deadline. The **UK** is not subject to AIFMD II (post-Brexit), though UK managers marketing into the EU must comply with the enhanced NPPR requirements.

### What happens if I don't comply by April 16?

Non-compliance with AIFMD II after April 16, 2026 exposes AIFMs to supervisory enforcement by their national competent authority. The specific consequences depend on the jurisdiction but may include: administrative fines, public censure, suspension of the AIFM's authorization, restrictions on marketing activities, and in severe cases, withdrawal of authorization. NCAs may also impose additional reporting requirements or on-site inspections. In practice, NCAs are likely to focus initial enforcement on the most visible requirements — Annex IV reporting compliance, LMT implementation, and delegation substance.

### Do sub-threshold AIFMs need to comply with AIFMD II?

Sub-threshold AIFMs (those managing below €100m with leverage or €500m without leverage and no redemption rights for 5 years) benefit from the lighter registration regime under Article 3 of the AIFMD. AIFMD II maintains this threshold but introduces enhanced registration requirements, including annual Annex IV reporting (at reduced frequency and scope) and compliance with certain provisions on conflicts of interest and reporting. The full LMT, delegation, and loan origination requirements do not apply to registered (non-authorized) AIFMs.

### How does AIFMD II affect private equity fund managers?

Private equity fund managers (managing closed-ended AIFs) are less affected by the LMT requirements (which primarily target open-ended funds) but must comply with: enhanced Annex IV reporting, the new delegation and substance requirements, improved investor disclosure (particularly on fees and costs — a historically sensitive area for PE), and (if applicable) the loan origination framework. PE managers should pay particular attention to the enhanced fee disclosure requirements under Article 23, which require granular breakdown of all costs borne by the fund.
