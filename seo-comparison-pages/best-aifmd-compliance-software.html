Best AIFMD II Compliance Software 2026 — Complete Buyer's Guide

March 2026 · 15 min read · Buyer's Guide

The AIFMD II deadline of April 16, 2026 is forcing every European AIFM to reevaluate their regulatory reporting infrastructure. The expanded Annex IV requirements — new fields for delegation, loan origination, liquidity management tools — make manual filing increasingly impractical.

This guide reviews 8 of the leading AIFMD compliance software platforms available in 2026. We've included pricing where publicly available, feature comparisons, and honest assessments of strengths and weaknesses. If a platform isn't listed here, it's either not focused on AIFMD reporting or lacks significant market presence in Europe.

Disclosure: This guide is published by Caelith, one of the platforms reviewed. We've made every effort to be fair and accurate in describing competitors. Readers trust honest reviews — biased comparisons get ignored. Where pricing or features are uncertain, we've said so.

The Platforms in Detail

1. Caelith

The only agent-first AIFMD compliance platform

What it is: Autonomous software agents that handle the entire Annex IV filing process — from data extraction to NCA portal submission. Built specifically for BaFin and CSSF with direct MVP Portal and eDesk integration.

Key features:

  • Fully autonomous filing — no human intervention required unless exceptions occur
  • Direct integration with BaFin MVP Portal and CSSF eDesk
  • AIFMD II support from day 1 (April 16, 2026)
  • REST API for programmatic access
  • Transparent per-fund pricing

Pricing: Per-fund, per-filing basis. Estimated €15,000–30,000/year for 10 funds filing quarterly.

Pros:

  • Highest automation level — eliminates manual filing work entirely
  • Transparent, predictable pricing
  • Purpose-built for EU NCA portals
  • Fast setup (days, not months)

Cons:

  • Newer platform — less track record than established players
  • Limited NCA coverage (currently BaFin and CSSF only)
  • AIFMD-focused — not a multi-regulation platform

Best for: EU-focused AIFMs that want maximum automation and transparent pricing.

2. FundApps

The enterprise standard for regulatory compliance

What it is: A comprehensive regulatory reporting platform covering AIFMD, MiFID, EMIR, SFTR, and dozens of other regimes globally. FundApps aggregates data and provides compliance dashboards, with human review before submission.

Key features:

  • Multi-regulation coverage (not just AIFMD)
  • Global NCA support (30+ jurisdictions)
  • Workflow-based approach with human oversight
  • Established vendor with large enterprise client base

Pricing: Enterprise licensing, not publicly disclosed. Estimated €40,000–80,000+/year based on AUM and regulatory modules.

Pros:

  • Proven platform with 15+ years in market
  • Broad regulatory coverage beyond AIFMD
  • Strong enterprise support and SLAs
  • Large compliance team and regular updates

Cons:

  • Requires manual portal submission (doesn't automate the final step)
  • Higher cost, especially for smaller AIFMs
  • Enterprise sales process — longer procurement cycles

Best for: Large, global asset managers with complex multi-regulation requirements.

3. FE fundinfo

Fund data and distribution platform with regulatory reporting

What it is: Primarily a fund data aggregation and distribution platform (connecting fund managers to distributors across Europe). Regulatory reporting is one module within a broader offering.

Key features:

  • Combined data distribution and regulatory reporting
  • Strong presence in European fund industry
  • ESMA-wide NCA coverage

Pricing: Custom, not publicly disclosed. Typically bundled with data distribution services.

Pros:

  • If you already use FE fundinfo for distribution, adding regulatory reporting is seamless
  • Broad European NCA coverage
  • Established fund industry vendor

Cons:

  • Regulatory reporting is a secondary offering, not the core product
  • Less automation than specialized platforms
  • Bundled pricing may be higher if you don't need distribution services

Best for: Fund managers already using FE fundinfo for data distribution.

4. Funds-Axis

Regulatory data and reporting platform for asset managers

What it is: A regulatory data platform that centralizes fund data and generates reports for multiple jurisdictions. Strong focus on transparency reporting (MiFID, EMIR) as well as AIFMD.

Key features:

  • Multi-regulation data aggregation
  • Global NCA coverage
  • Integration with major fund administrators

Pricing: Custom enterprise pricing. Estimated €30,000–60,000+/year for mid-sized managers.

Pros:

  • Good for managers filing across multiple regulations
  • Strong integrations with fund administrators
  • Decent global NCA coverage

Cons:

  • Workflow-based, not fully automated
  • Less specialized in AIFMD than niche players
  • Enterprise pricing can be opaque

Best for: Asset managers with multi-regulation filing needs beyond just AIFMD.

5. Matterhorn Regulatory Solutions

Swiss compliance suite for fund managers

What it is: A comprehensive compliance platform covering AIFMD, UCITS, and Swiss fund regulations. Particularly strong in the Swiss and Liechtenstein markets.

Key features:

  • Full compliance suite (not just reporting)
  • Strong Swiss/Liechtenstein expertise
  • Risk management and compliance monitoring tools

Pricing: Enterprise licensing, estimated €50,000–100,000+/year depending on modules.

Pros:

  • Comprehensive compliance platform, not just reporting
  • Strong in Swiss market
  • Good for managers needing broader compliance tools

Cons:

  • Higher cost due to broader feature set
  • May be overkill if you only need AIFMD reporting
  • AIFMD II updates on roadmap but timing unclear

Best for: Swiss/Liechtenstein fund managers needing comprehensive compliance infrastructure.

6. DataTracks

XBRL and regulatory filing specialists

What it is: A managed service provider specializing in XBRL filings and regulatory reports. DataTracks prepares and submits filings on behalf of clients — outsourcing model rather than software-only.

Key features:

  • Managed service approach — DataTracks handles the work
  • Strong XBRL expertise
  • Global regulatory filing experience

Pricing: Project-based, not subscription. Varies widely based on engagement scope.

Pros:

  • Fully managed — minimal internal effort required
  • Deep XBRL expertise (useful for some NCA portals)
  • Flexible engagement model

Cons:

  • Higher cost for ongoing service vs self-service software
  • Less control and transparency than in-house platforms
  • Dependency on external service provider

Best for: Managers who prefer to fully outsource regulatory filing.

7. anevis solutions

German compliance specialists with managed service model

What it is: A German firm offering compliance software + managed services. Combines proprietary tools with expert consulting. Strong presence in the DACH (Germany, Austria, Switzerland) market.

Key features:

  • Software + human expertise hybrid
  • DACH-market focus with BaFin, CSSF, FMA knowledge
  • Active AIFMD II updates

Pricing: Project-based + ongoing service fees. Estimated €30,000–80,000/year depending on engagement.

Pros:

  • Deep regulatory expertise in German/Austrian markets
  • Managed service reduces internal workload
  • Established vendor with good DACH references

Cons:

  • Higher cost due to service component
  • Less automation than pure-software approaches
  • Geographic focus limits global applicability

Best for: German/Austrian KVGs seeking managed service with regional expertise.

8. ARKK

EU compliance platform for investment firms

What it is: A compliance workflow platform covering MiFID, AIFMD, and other EU regulations. Focuses on streamlining compliance processes for investment firms.

Key features:

  • Workflow automation for compliance processes
  • Multi-regulation support
  • EU-focused

Pricing: Custom pricing, not publicly disclosed.

Pros:

  • Good for firms needing broader compliance workflow tools
  • EU-focused with understanding of local requirements

Cons:

  • Less specialized in AIFMD Annex IV filing
  • AIFMD II support timeline unclear
  • Limited public information on features and pricing

Best for: Investment firms needing broader compliance workflow automation beyond AIFMD.

How to Choose

Decision Framework

1. What's your regulatory footprint?

  • EU-only (BaFin, CSSF) → Caelith or anevis
  • Global (10+ jurisdictions) → FundApps or Funds-Axis
  • DACH-focused → anevis or Matterhorn

2. How much do you want to automate?

  • Fully autonomous → Caelith
  • Workflow assistance → FundApps, Funds-Axis, ARKK
  • Fully outsourced → DataTracks, anevis

3. What's your budget?

  • <€30k/year → Caelith
  • €30k–80k/year → anevis, Funds-Axis, FundApps
  • €80k+/year → FundApps (enterprise), Matterhorn, DataTracks

4. Do you need more than AIFMD reporting?

  • AIFMD only → Caelith, anevis
  • Multi-regulation → FundApps, Funds-Axis, Matterhorn, ARKK

Final Thoughts

There is no single "best" AIFMD compliance software. The right choice depends on your regulatory footprint, automation preferences, budget, and risk tolerance.

What is clear: the days of manually building XML files and uploading to NCA portals are ending. AIFMD II's expanded data requirements make automation essential. The question is whether you automate with agents (Caelith), workflows (FundApps, Funds-Axis), or outsource entirely (DataTracks, anevis).

Whichever path you choose, start the evaluation process now. The April 16, 2026 deadline is less than 6 weeks away, and onboarding a new platform takes time.
