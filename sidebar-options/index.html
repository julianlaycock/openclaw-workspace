Sidebar Redesign — 3 Options

Each option rethinks the information architecture for a compliance dashboard with 15+ pages.

Option A — Flat & Clean

Minimal Scannable

Flatten everything. No collapsibles, no sub-items. Two clear groups separated by intent: daily work vs compliance & reporting. Every page is one click away.

Design rationale:
  • 10 items total — every page is visible without scrolling
  • No expand/collapse = zero cognitive overhead
  • "Rules & Screening" and "Regulatory Reports" combine related sub-pages (tab navigation on the page itself)
  • Holdings, Onboarding, Annex IV, Evidence Bundle move to in-page tabs — they're contextual, not top-level navigation
  • 2 groups by mental model: "what I manage" vs "what regulates it"

Option B — Icon Rail + Panel

Pro UX Scalable

Left icon rail for categories, expandable panel for sub-pages. Collapses to just icons for maximum content space. Inspired by Linear, Notion, VS Code.

Design rationale:
  • Icon rail = persistent wayfinding, always visible even collapsed
  • Flyout panel shows contextual sub-pages per active category
  • Scales infinitely — add new categories without cluttering
  • Collapsible: click logo or press [ to hide panel, leaving just 52px rail
  • Red dot on Readiness = attention signal without text
  • Best for power users who navigate between many sections frequently

Option C — Smart Hierarchy

Balanced Familiar

Restructured two-level nav with smarter groupings. Only 5 top-level items — everything else is one expand away. Familiar pattern, better organized.

Design rationale:
  • 5 top-level items (down from 9) — sidebar feels light and intentional
  • Grouping by entity not action: "Funds & Assets" holds everything fund-related, "Investors" holds everything investor-related
  • Screening moves under Investors (it's investor-level) — more logical
  • Calendar moves under Reporting (it's compliance deadlines) — not standalone
  • Dashboard + Audit Log are ungrouped — they're cross-cutting
  • Active section auto-expands, others collapse — keeps sidebar short

My recommendation: Option C for now — it fixes the clutter without over-engineering. Option B is the long-term play if we add more modules (AML, SFDR, ELTIF). Option A is safest for demos but limits growth.
